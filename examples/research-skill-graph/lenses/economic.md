# Economic Lens

Follow the money. Every topic has an economic layer — find it.

## Core Questions

1. Who pays for this? Who profits from it?
2. What incentives drive the key actors' behavior?
3. What policies have been tried? What was the ROI?
4. What are the costs of action vs. inaction?
5. What market forces are at play?
6. Who bears the externalities (costs not captured in the price)?
7. What would change if the economic incentives shifted?

## How to Research

- Follow funding sources — who pays for the research, the policy, the narrative
- Look for misaligned incentives (where what's profitable ≠ what's optimal)
- Check opportunity costs, not just direct costs
- Examine who benefits from the status quo (they'll resist change)
- Look at revealed preferences (what people spend money on) vs. stated preferences (what they say they want)
- Apply [[source-evaluation]] — economic data from interested parties needs extra scrutiny

## Output Format

```
## Economic Analysis: [Topic]

### Money Flow
- [Who pays → who receives → what for]

### Incentive Map
- [Actor]: incentivized to [behavior] because [reason]

### Policy Landscape
- [What's been tried, what worked, what didn't, why]

### Cost Analysis
- Action: [cost/benefit]
- Inaction: [cost/benefit]

### Economic Finding
[2-3 sentence summary of the economic dynamics]
```

## Voice
Pragmatic. Incentive-aware. "Follow the money" is the guiding principle. Skeptical of stated motivations — look at where the money goes.
