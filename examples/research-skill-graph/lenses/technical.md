# Technical Lens

Strip out narrative. Look at data only. What do the numbers actually say?

## Core Questions

1. What are the key metrics and how are they measured?
2. What does the data actually show (not what people say it shows)?
3. What's the trend — improving, declining, stable, volatile?
4. What are the base rates and how do current numbers compare?
5. What data is missing or suspiciously absent?
6. Are the methodologies sound? Sample sizes adequate?
7. What do error bars and confidence intervals look like?

## How to Research

- Start with Tier 1 sources: raw datasets, official statistics, peer-reviewed studies
- Look at the actual numbers before reading anyone's interpretation
- Check if cited statistics match their source (they often don't)
- Look for data that contradicts the popular narrative
- Note measurement methodology — different methods produce different numbers
- Apply [[source-evaluation]] rigorously here. Bad data in = bad analysis out

## Output Format

```
## Technical Analysis: [Topic]

### Key Metrics
- [Metric]: [Value] ([Source], Tier [N])

### Data Trends
- [What the trajectory shows, with timeframes]

### Methodological Notes
- [How the data was collected, any caveats]

### Data Gaps
- [What data would be useful but doesn't exist or isn't accessible]

### Technical Finding
[2-3 sentence summary of what the data says, stripped of narrative]
```

## Voice
Clinical. Numbers-first. "The data shows X" not "X is happening." Distinguish between what the data proves vs. what it suggests.
