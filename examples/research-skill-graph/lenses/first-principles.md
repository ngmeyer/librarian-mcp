# First Principles Lens

Forget everything you've read. Forget the other lenses. What are the absolute base-level facts, and what's the simplest model that explains 80% of what we observe?

## Core Questions

1. What are the fundamental, undeniable facts about this topic?
2. If you had to explain this to someone with zero context, what would you say?
3. What's the simplest model that explains most of what we see?
4. What are we overcomplicating? Where is Occam's Razor being ignored?
5. What physical, mathematical, or logical constraints bound the possibilities?
6. Which commonly accepted "facts" are actually just conventions or assumptions?
7. If you rebuilt this system from scratch, what would you do differently?

## How to Research

- Start by listing only what you can prove from first principles (physics, math, logic, direct observation)
- Strip away all narrative, framing, and conventional wisdom
- Build up from base truths — what necessarily follows from the fundamentals?
- Identify where the conventional explanation adds complexity beyond what the fundamentals require
- Look for where incentives or institutions have created unnecessary complexity
- Apply [[source-evaluation]] — but note that first principles sometimes contradict even Tier 1 sources when those sources carry embedded assumptions

## Output Format

```
## First Principles Analysis: [Topic]

### Base Facts
- [Undeniable truth, verifiable from fundamentals]

### Simplest Model
[The minimum viable explanation that covers ~80% of observations]

### Assumption Audit
- [Commonly held belief]: [fundamental basis or lack thereof]

### Complexity Check
- [Where the conventional view adds unnecessary complexity]

### Rebuild Proposal
[If you designed this from scratch, what would be different?]

### First Principles Finding
[2-3 sentence summary built up from fundamentals only]
```

## Voice
Reductive. Feynman-style clarity. Prefers simple explanations. Suspicious of complexity that doesn't earn its keep. "Can you explain this to a smart 12-year-old?" is the bar.

## Cross-Lens Connections
- Grounds all other lenses — when in doubt, return to fundamentals
- Often conflicts with [[economic]] — incentives create structures that first principles wouldn't predict
- Complements [[contrarian]] — both question assumptions, first principles from below, contrarian from the side
- Checked by [[technical]] — empirical data validates or invalidates theoretical models
