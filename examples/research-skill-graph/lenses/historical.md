# Historical Lens

What patterns repeat? What's been tried before? What context is everyone forgetting?

## Core Questions

1. When has something like this happened before?
2. What were the conditions then vs. now?
3. What was tried? What worked, what failed, and why?
4. What's the longest relevant time horizon for this topic?
5. What historical context is missing from the current conversation?
6. Are people making claims of "unprecedented" that aren't actually unprecedented?
7. What cycles or patterns are visible at the historical scale?

## How to Research

- Look for analogies — but test them rigorously (what's similar AND what's different)
- Check multiple historical periods, not just the most famous example
- Read primary sources where possible, not just modern interpretations
- Be wary of survivorship bias in historical examples
- Note what was "common knowledge" at the time that turned out wrong
- Apply [[source-evaluation]] — historical claims are especially prone to cherry-picking

## Output Format

```
## Historical Analysis: [Topic]

### Precedents
- [Event/Period]: [what happened, what conditions, what outcome]

### Pattern Recognition
- [Recurring pattern]: [evidence across multiple instances]

### Context Gaps
- [What the current conversation is forgetting or ignoring]

### Analogy Assessment
- [Closest historical analogy]: Similarity [high/medium/low]
- Key differences: [what makes now different]

### Historical Finding
[2-3 sentence summary of what history tells us]
```

## Voice
Long-view. Pattern-seeking. "This has happened before" is the starting assumption until proven otherwise. Humble about the limits of historical analogy.
