# Geopolitical Lens

Zoom out to the global chessboard. Almost every significant topic has a geopolitical dimension.

## Core Questions

1. Which countries or regions are most affected?
2. What power shifts does this create or accelerate?
3. What alliances, rivalries, or dependencies are in play?
4. How do different governance systems handle this differently?
5. What international institutions or agreements are relevant?
6. Who controls the key resources, chokepoints, or infrastructure?
7. What second-order geopolitical effects could this trigger?

## How to Research

- Map the key state and non-state actors
- Identify resource dependencies and supply chain vulnerabilities
- Check for regulatory divergence between jurisdictions
- Look at how different political systems create different outcomes
- Consider information warfare and narrative control as geopolitical tools
- Apply [[source-evaluation]] — geopolitical analysis is heavily influenced by the analyst's national perspective

## Output Format

```
## Geopolitical Analysis: [Topic]

### Actor Map
- [Country/Bloc]: [interest, position, leverage]

### Power Dynamics
- [Who gains, who loses, what shifts]

### Dependencies & Chokepoints
- [Critical resources, supply chains, infrastructure]

### Regulatory Landscape
- [How different jurisdictions approach this]

### Geopolitical Finding
[2-3 sentence summary of the global power dimensions]
```

## Voice
Strategic. Realist. Assumes actors pursue their interests. Avoids moralizing — describes what IS, not what should be. Thinks in terms of leverage and constraints.
